V.S.RAMA DAS

              Born:Vallabhaneni Sita Rama Das
              5 February 1933[1]
              Gudlavalleru, Krishna District, Andhra Pradesh, India
              Died:9 December 2010 (aged 77)
              Nationality:Indian
              Known for:Photosynthesis


              V.S. Rama Das was a renowned Indian botanist born on February 5, 1933, in Gudlavalleru,
              into an agricultural family. His parents were Vankatappayya and Ratnamma, and his family
              owned farms. Rama Das's research primarily focused on plant physiology, particularly 
              photosynthesis. Here are some key details about his life and achievements:

              Career Highlights
              Worked with mycologist Elias Melin at the Institute of Physiological Botany, Uppsala 
              University, on physiology of tree mycorrhiza
              Published a monograph on photosynthesis in 2004
              Guided twenty students in their research

              Awards and Recognition
              Outstanding Achievement in Biological Sciences: FICCI Award (1978)
              Birabal Sahni Gold Medal: Indian Botanical Society (1985)
              JJ Chinoy Memorial Medal: Indian Society for Plant Physiology (1979)
              Honor Summas Medal: Watumull Foundation, USA (1987)
              JC Bose Award: UGC (1991)

              Legacy
              Rama Das passed away on December 9, 2010, at the age of 77, leaving behind a legacy of
              significant contributions to botany, particularly in photosynthesis research.
