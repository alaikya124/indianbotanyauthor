M.S.SWAMINATHAN

            
             Birth Date: August 7, 1925
             Birthplace: Kumbakonam, Thanjavur District, Madras Presidency, British India (present-day Tamil Nadu, India)
             Family: His mother was Parvati Thangammal, and he had a strong connection with his family, donating one-third of their 2000-acre land to Vinoba Bhave's cause
             Education: Swaminathan completed his Bachelor's degree in zoology, followed by a Master's degree in botany and a Ph.D. in genetics from the University of Cambridge
             Marriage: He married Mina Swaminathan on April 11, 1955, and had three daughters with her. Mina passed away in 2022.
           
           
             Cytogenetics: He conducted extensive research on the cytogenetics of hexaploid wheat and elucidated the
            origin and evolutionary processes of potatoes as an autotetraploid.
            Plant Breeding: Swaminathan worked on crop improvement, developing high-yielding varieties of wheat and 
            rice that played a crucial role in India's Green Revolution.
            Genetics: His research focused on the genus Solanum, with specific attention to potatoes, and explored 
            species differentiation and polyploidy.

            Some notable publications by Swaminathan include :

           "Major Flowering Trees of Tropical Gardens" (2019) with S.L. Kochhar
           "Notes on induced polyploids in the tuber-bearing Solanum species and
            their crossability with Solanum tuberosum" (1951) in the American Potato Journal
           "The cytology of haploid plants of Solanum demissum" (1953) with H.W. Howard

            Swaminathan's work had a profound impact on Indian agriculture, and he is widely recognized as the "Father
            of the Green Revolution in India". His contributions to botany and plant breeding continue to inspire research
